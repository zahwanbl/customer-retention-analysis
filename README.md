# 📊 Customer Value & Retention Analysis

This project analyzes customer behavior and retention patterns using the Olist E-commerce dataset. The goal is to identify key customer segments, evaluate repeat purchase behavior, and uncover opportunities to improve customer retention.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Identify one-time vs repeat customers
- Analyze customer value and revenue contribution
- Segment customers using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)
- Provide actionable business recommendations

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Skills

- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib)
- SQL (data querying logic)
- Data Cleaning & Transformation
- Customer Segmentation (RFM)
- Business Insight & Storytelling

---

## 📂 Dataset

- Olist Brazilian E-commerce Public Dataset
- Tables used:
  - `olist_customers_dataset`
  - `olist_orders_dataset`
  - `olist_order_payments_dataset`

---

## 🔍 Methodology

1. Data cleaning and preprocessing
2. Merging multiple datasets (customers, orders, payments)
3. Aggregating data at customer level
4. Calculating:
   - Total orders
   - Total spending
   - First & last purchase
5. Performing RFM-based segmentation
6. Analyzing customer retention patterns

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- **97.13%** of customers are one-time buyers
- Only **2.87%** are repeat customers
- A small group of customers contributes significantly to revenue
- High-value one-time customers represent a major retention opportunity

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Target high-value one-time customers with personalized campaigns
- Implement loyalty programs to increase repeat purchases
- Improve post-purchase engagement strategies
- Focus on converting occasional buyers into long-term customers
